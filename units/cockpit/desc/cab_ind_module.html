Cabin Indication Module (CIM)

will come on if the cabin Cabin altitude is more than 9,800 feet.

The cabin indication module is located in the flight compartment on the overhead panel and displays differential pressure, cabin altitude and cabin altitude rate of change.
The cabin indication module is interfaced with the CPC and ADU's for differential, altitude and rate indications. The differential pressure indicator gives a differential pressure from 0.5 to 5.8 ± 0.15 psi (-3.45 to 40 ±1.03 kPa) between the cabin pressure and the outside static pressure.

An internal absolute cabin pressure sensor performs cabin altitude indication. This sensor's information is transmitted on an ARINC 429 data bus to the CPC. This pressure sensor also gives the excessive altitude warning to the Cabin Pressure Controller and the CABIN PRESS warning light.

NOTE: The CABIN PRESS warning light on the Caution and Warning panel comes on when the cabin altitude reaches 10,000 ft.

      • Used as a guide to set cabin differential pressure when using the MAN DIFF switch in MAN mode.

The interfaces for the pressurization system are:
  • Power Lever Angle (60 Micro Switch) right lever only.
  • PSEU (WOW)
  • ESC Controller
  • IFC 1 (CDS)
  • ADU1 and ADU2
  • Caution and Warning System (CABIN PRESS)
  • 28Vdc Left Main Bus( Cabin Press Auto Cont circuit breaker).
