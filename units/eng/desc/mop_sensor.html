Oil Pressure Transducer (MOP)

During engine operation, oil enters the inlet port and pushes on the pressure sensing element. The element converts the oil pressure to an electrical signal.

Air from the No. 5 bearing cavity enters the reference port and pushes on the second pressure sensing element. This element converts the air pressure to an electrical signal.

The two electrical signals are processed by the electrical circuitry and a signal is output and transmitted through the electrical wiring harness to the IFC for display in the flight compartment.
