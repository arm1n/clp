Intake Bypass Door

The intake bypass door opens and ejects ice (which could damage the engine) to atmosphere. The intake bypass FOD door assembly is located at the rear of the duct. It contains an actuator and a door.

The door is in the lower skin and has a hinge on the aft. The actuator opens the door and ejects ice to atmosphere. The air intake is part of the engine nacelle. The air intake lets large quantities of air enter the engine for use in all engine operations. A foreign object debris (FOD) duct is installed in the intake system. The FOD duct moves debris and foreign objects away from the engine compressor. The debris and foreign objects are ejected through the FOD door which is installed on the bottom surface of the lower cowl.
