TCAS

The TCAS II interrogates other aircraft's transponders and uses their replies to calculate the parameters of the intruding aircraft that follow:
  • Range
  • Relative bearing
  • Altitude and vertical speed
  • Closure rate

The TCAS II compares this data with its own position to calculate a potential collision condition, and give an audio and visual indication for vertical avoidance maneuvers. NOTE: Note: TCAS II is unable to detect any intruding aircraft without an operating Air Traffic Control Radar Beacon System (ATCRBS) transponder (operating in Mode A and C) or a Mode S transponder.

The Electronic Instrument System (EIS) shows the TCAS II parameters that follow:
  • Non-Threat Traffic
  • Proximity Intruder Traffic
  • Traffic Advisory (TA)
  • Resolution Advisory (RA)
Traffic Advisory (TA): The TA indication shows the intruding aircraft's parameters that follow:
  • Relative position
  • Altitude
  • A trend arrow to show if it is climbing or descending at speed more than 500 ft/min (52.4 m/min)
