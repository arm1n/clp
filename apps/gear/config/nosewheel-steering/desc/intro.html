STEERING SYSTEM - General Description

The steering system gives directional control of the aircraft while on the ground and supplies shimmy damping of the nosewheels. The STEERING switch in the flight compartment sets the steering system to the power ON mode or the power OFF mode.

Hydraulic powered shimmy damping is supplied by the steering manifold when the steering system is in the power ON mode. Passive, free castor shimmy damping of the nosewheels is supplied by the nosewheel centering system when the pilot select switch is in the power OFF mode.

If any component in the steering system fails, the nosewheel centering system will supply passive, free caster shimmy damping of the nosewheels. The passive, free castor mode lets the nosewheels steer ±120° to the left and to the right of the center position.
