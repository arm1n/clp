The nose landing gear (NLG) lets the forward fuselage absorb the shock of landing and gives stability and direction during aircraft taxi. The NLG doors enclose the retracted NLG in the forward fuselage and add to the aerodynamic efficiency of the aircraft.

The NLG is installed in the wheelwell in the nose fuselage, located forward of the forward pressure bulkhead. When the NLG retracts the forward and aft doors enclose it in the wheelwell. A taxi light is installed on the lower right side of the outer cylinder of the shock strut. The GROUND LOCK control handle operates a cable system to engage the downlock safety lever.
