PROPELLERS ANTI−ICING

Ice detection is not available for the propeller antiicing system, therefore to avoid excessive build−up of ice, the pilot must select propeller heat ON when flying through known icing conditions.

When heat is selected ON, all six blades on one propeller are heated at one time, then the propeller on the other side is heated.

The TMCU performs continuous passive fault monitoring of the propeller deice system. An active pilot-initiated test mode is available to support preflight functional test. Nominal blade heater resistance is measured during a manually initiated calibration sequence and recorded in Non-Volatile Memory (NVM).

The calibration data is used during the passive fault monitoring and pre-flight functional test to monitor the blade heater elements. A fault indicator output discrete signal indicates when a system fault is sensed by the TMCU.
The isolation contactor drive signal is disabled by the TMCU when a fault (which limits continued safe operation of the system) is sensed. All detected faults are recorded in NVM.
The propeller deicing system monitors the propeller speed (Np) and Total Air Temperature (TAT).

The system interfaces with:
  • Integrated Flight Cabinet
  • FADEC Local
  • Proximity sensing Electronic Unit (PSEU)
  • TMCU remote
