AIRFRAME PNEUMATIC DEICING SYSTEMS

Bleed air from the engines is supplied to the pneumatic de−icing boots to shed ice accumulation from the critical surfaces which would otherwise decrease aircraft performance.

Nineteen pneumatically inflatable rubber de−icing boots are bonded to the airframe structure on the wing and tail leading edges. Rubber blankets (boots) cover the area where ice accumulation is anticipated.

Within these boots are narrow tubes that can be inflated with compressed air.
