FLIGHT CONTROLS CAUTION LIGHTS

ROLL SPOILER SYSTEM This light will come on when the flight control electronic control unit (FCECU) senses that the inboard spoiler lift-dump valves have not operated correctly. This condition can cause the spoilers to extend or retract at the incorrect time This light will come on during these conditions:
  • The flight control electronic control unit (FCECU) is not able to control the outboard spoiler lift dump operation
  • The outboard spoilers do not extend at touchdown
  • The outboard spoilers are extended after the taxi mode selection
 caution light comes on for the conditions that follow:
  • The flight control electronic control unit (FCECU) does not prevent outboard spoilers operation when the airspeed is more than 185 kt (42.62 km/h)
  • The FCECU prevents outboard spoilers operation when the airspeed is less than 150 kt (277.8 km/h)
  • The airspeed is invalid
 The CAWP ROLL SPOILER INBD HYD caution light comes on when the inboard spoiler hydraulic system pressure is low. The CAWP ROLL SPLR OUTBD HYD caution light comes on when the No. 2 hydraulic system pressure is less than 900 psi and the flight control electronic control unit (FCECU) senses that the airspeed is less than 165 kt (305.58 km/h).
ELEVATOR SYSTEM An ELEVATOR ASYMMETRY caution light comes on when there is a difference between the left and right elevator positions The ELEV FEEL caution light, controlled by the FCECU, comes on if:- Left or right Pitch Feel Actuator fails- Airspeed No.1 and Airspeed No.2 disagree- Acceleration No.1 and Acceleration No.2 disagree. An ELEVATOR PRESS caution light comes on if all three systems are supplying power to the elevator actuators. The #3 HYD PUMP caution light comes on if the #3 Hyd system pressure falls to 900 psi (6205 kPa), or the pump has been operating for longer than 60 seconds on the ground.
FLAP SYSTEM The CAWP FLAP POWER caution light is latched on and no more flap movement is possible when the flap control unit (FCU) senses a condition that follows:
  • Flap asymmetry
  • Uncommanded movement
  • A position transducer has malfunctioned
  • The flap lever transducer has malfunctioned
  • The arming switch has malfunctioned
  • The FCU has malfunctioned
There are two possible conditions that can cause the FLAP POWER caution light to come on as nuisance fault:
  • The FLAP POWER caution light will come on if you move the flap control lever to a different position and there is no pressure in the No. 1 hydraulic system for six seconds.
  • The FLAP POWER caution light will come on if you move the flap control lever to a different position and the release trigger does not go to the bottom of the detent for 10 seconds.
 The CAWP FLAP DRIVE caution light comes on when the flap control unit (FCU) senses a condition that follows:
  • Hydraulic pressure is sensed with no flap selection (the shut−off valve stays open after the flap movement is completed)
  • The flap panels have stopped moving (stalled) before reaching the set position (a difference between flap position selection and flap position indication is sensed)
  • The FCU has malfunctioned.
NOTE: The flap system will continue to operate in a degraded mode when the FLAP.
RUDDER SYSTEM If hydraulic failure does occur (>350 psi), the related No. 1 RUD HYD or No.2 RUD HYD caution light comes on.These caution lights also come on if the FCECU has shut down one of the hydraulic systems due to valve malfunction or if either RUD 1 or RUD 2 switchlight is pushed. The RUD CNTRL caution light comes on if:
  • FCECU detects a difference of 20 knots (37.06 km/h) or more between the Airspeed 1 and Airspeed 2 from the Air Data Units
  • FCECU loses its ability to limit hydraulic pressure - Both hydraulic systems fail
STALL SYSTEM The CAWP #1 STALL SYST FAIL caution light comes on for the malfunction conditions that follow:
  • Stall Protection Module (SPM) 1 ((SPM) 2) is defective
  • SPM1 (SPM2) does not receive valid data from SPM2 (SPM1)
  • The No. 1 (No. 2) stick shaker is defective
  • AOA2 (AOA1) is defective or not heated
  • Icing discrete input is defective
  • Operational test (STALL WARN TEST 1 (STALL WARN TEST 2)) has sensed a caution light or stick shaker discrepancy
  • Flap Position Indicator Unit (FPIU) left and right flap position inputs are defective
  • Air Data Unit (ADU) 1 and ADU2 mach inputs are defective
  • Full Authority Digital Electronic Control (FADEC) 1 and FADEC2 engine torque inputs are defective
  • Stick shaker and opposite SPM are defective
 The PUSHER SYST FAIL caution light comes on when a stick pusher SHUT OFF switchlight selection is made.
TRIM SYSTEM The PITCH TRIM caution light, controlled by the FCECU, comes on if:
  • One pitch trim motor, or its control card fails
  • Pitch trim “runaway” is sensed
  • Airspeed No.1 disagrees with Airspeed No.2
  • Pitch trim input commands disagree
