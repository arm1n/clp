Component Location Index for the Aircraft Zones

AMM TASK 06-30-00-992-801 (TR-AMM)

Temporary Allowance for Continued Operation with Missing Sealant on the External Access Panels
AMM TASK 51-23-00-390-870 (TR-AMM)


Identification Method

Each access panel, door, or fairing has a two−group identification code that indicates its position on the aircraft.

1. A Three−digit Numeric Group

This three−digit numeric group identifies the Major zone/Major sub−zone/unit zone where the access panels, doors, or fairings are located.

  • Zoning data is supplied to make the location of work areas and components easier. The aircraft is divided into eight major zones numbered from 100 to 800, in increments of 100. Each major zone is subdivided into major sub-zones and unit zones.
  • Major sub−zones are subdivisions of the major zones. Each major sub−zone is identified by the second digit of the three digit number.
  • Unit zones are subdivisions of the major sub−zones. Each unit zone is identified by the third digit of the three digit number.

2. A Two−digit Letter Group
This two−digit letter group gives the position of the access panel, door, or fairing in relation to the aircraft as follows:

NOTE: The letters “I” and “O” are not used.

FIRST LETTER The first letter identifies the general position of the access door, panel or fairing on theaircraft. This starts with the letter “A” and goes from inboard to outboard and from front to rear. SECOND LETTER The second letter identifies the location of the access door, panel or fairing in relation to the aircraft as follows:
  • T = Top
  • B = Bottom
  • L = Left Side
  • R = Right Side
