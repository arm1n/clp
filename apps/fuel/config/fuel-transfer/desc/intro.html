FUEL TRANSFER


Operational Check of the Fuel Transfer System AMM TASK 28-23-00-710-801 (TR-AMM)

The fuel transfer system moves fuel from one tank to the other to correct fuel imbalances or for fuel management. The system can empty one fuel tank for maintenance without defueling the aircraft.

Fuel can be transferred when the aircraft is in the air or on the ground. Fuel transfer is controlled through the FUEL CONTROL TRANSFER panel. The fuel transfer system shares some of its components with the refuel/defuel system. Fuel transfer cannot occur during the refuel/defuel process. The fuel transfer system uses the auxiliary pump, part of the engine feed system, and the refuel/defuel/transfer manifold.

Fuel is transferred by the auxiliary pump of the donor tank.

Fuel transfer data is shown in the flight compartment.
