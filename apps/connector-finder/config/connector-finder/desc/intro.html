MAINTENANCE PRACTICES

Cleaning of Electrical Connector, Switch or Relay

The procedure that follows approves the use of lubricants for preventing corrosion and to remove moisture from electrical connectors, switches and relays.

 AMM TASK 20-30-00-110-801 (TR-AMM)

Electrical Test of the Aircraft Wiring

The maintenance procedure that follows is for the electrical test of the aircraft wiring. This procedure includes testing procedures for the wiring continuity and isolation check to verify the integrity of electrical wiring with an ohmmeter and a mega ohmmeter (high voltage insulation tester or Megger), DC voltage check, AC voltage check, and bonding test.

 AMM TASK 20-30-11-760-801 (TR-AMM)

REPAIR OF THE METAL OVERBRAIDING SHIELD ON ELECTRICAL CABLE HARNESSES

The procedure that follows is for the repair of metal overbraid shielding on electrical cable harnesses. Two methods are given. Method A is the preferred method. Method B may be used in areas where repairs are difficult with a solid phenolic tube.

 WM TASK 20-15-03-201 (TR-WM)

WIRING − MAINTENANCE PRACTICES

This section gives instructions on the procedures required to maintain, repair or install a wiring assembly on an aircraft.

 WM TASK 20-12-10-201 (TR-WM)

SHIELDING − REMOVAL/INSTALLATION

This section includes general requirements and instructions for terminating electrical shields.

 WM TASK 20-15-00-401 (TR-WM)

SPLICING OF ELECTRICAL WIRE

This section contains the procedure and requirements for the splicing of electrical wires and cable assemblies in the aircraft fuselage and wing area only. This procedure is necessary to maintain the designed reliability of the aircraft electrical wire and cable assemblies.

 WM TASK 20-16-05-201 (TR-WM)

WIRE IDENTIFICATION

The maintenance procedures that follow are for the identification of electrical wires and cables used to connect electrical equipment.

 WM TASK 20-10-00-201 (TR-WM)

PROCEDURES FOR DISCONNECTION AND CONNECTION OF ELECTRICAL CONNECTOR(S) − FOR RETURN TO SERVICE OF THE AIRCRAFT

This section gives instructions on procedures to removed and re−connected connector(s) for troubleshooting, access and for the repair and maintenance of the connector(s) installed in the aircraft. This procedure applies to all the wiring in the aircraft through the disconnected connector(s).
